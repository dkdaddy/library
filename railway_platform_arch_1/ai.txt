Railway Platform Arch — Image Description (IMG_0937–IMG_0940)

The images show a recessed arch in a Victorian railway bridge abutment,
used as a sheltered waiting area on a station platform. The structure is
built entirely from yellow London stock brick, now aged and darkened with
soot, damp staining, and patches of green moss/algae growth, especially
in the upper corners and joints.

IMG_0937 — Brick wall detail (close-up):
A close-up of the back wall brickwork inside the recess. The bricks are
yellow London stock, laid in a stretcher bond pattern. The mortar joints
are grey and somewhat eroded in places. The surface shows heavy weathering:
darker sooty discolouration in patches, with the lower courses appearing
more worn and gritty. Sunlight catches the upper-right corner of the
frame. The left edge of the image shows the dark return where the back
wall meets a side pier.

IMG_0938 — Floor/ground detail (close-up):
A downward-looking shot of the platform floor at the base of the arch.
The surface is grey concrete paving slabs, worn smooth and slightly dirty.
A diagonal joint/crack runs across the lower-right portion of the frame.
The edge of a white-painted element (possibly a bench leg or platform
edging) is visible at the top-left, along with the base of what appears
to be a grey metal bench support at the top-right.

IMG_0939 — Right-side view showing pier and arch:
A wider view taken from inside the recess, looking toward the right-hand
pier. Two modern wooden-slatted benches with grey powder-coated steel
frames sit against the back wall on the left side of the frame. The
right-hand brick pier is clearly visible — it has a wider base section
that steps in via a short angled/chamfered transition to the narrower
main shaft above. A circular "Help Point" intercom unit is mounted on
the pier face. The arch springs from the top of the pier, curving
overhead; the brickwork of the arch soffit is visible at the top of the
frame. The back wall visibly rakes/leans inward as it rises. Some moss
and damp staining is visible in the upper corner where the arch meets
the back wall. A corrugated metal canopy or platform roof edge is
visible at the top-right. On the floor, a yellow tactile/accessibility
marking is partially visible at bottom-right.

IMG_0940 — Left-side view showing pier and arch:
A similar view but from the opposite side, looking toward the left-hand
pier. The pier is well-lit by sunlight, clearly showing the three-part
profile: a wider rectangular base, a short chamfered/angled transition
band, and the narrower main pillar shaft above. A small red-and-white
"no smoking" sign is fixed to the pier face. The arch curves overhead
from the top of the pier, with the barrel-vaulted brick soffit visible
receding into the depth of the recess. The back wall brickwork is
darker and more soot-stained than the sunlit pier. Two wooden-slatted
benches with grey steel frames are visible at the bottom-right, tucked
into the recess. The corrugated metal platform canopy is visible at the
top-left corner.
